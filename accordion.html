Accordion

Project Manager & Designer

I was project manager and a designer for a beta version of Accordion, an app to facilitate document sharing and communication. Gist Digital, the incubator working with Accordion, had produced an alpha version of the native mobile app. I was tasked with leading a team through an iteration of the alpha mobile app that Gist Digital, the incubator working with Accordion, produced, and creating a corresponding web app.

Through user testing and industry research, my team was able to hone in on the most appropriate target audience, iterate on the mobile app, and design a corresponding web application to make document sharing and communication easier. As project manager, I was responsible for leading a team of four-- one marketer and two other designers-- through this process.

User Testing

I tackled the app's biggest challenge first: narrowing its target audience. Its original audience was unfocused and broad, which made it difficult to optimize it to any one user base. To solve this issue, I led my group through market research and user testing to determine which segment of the construciton industry would benefit the most from this app. This allowed us to then optimize the app to this user base, and iterate on the app in a way that better served this audience.

To identify the app's target audience and then determine what features to change, my team first identified the most important information to glean from each interview: is the app applicable to the interviewee's life? Is it functional? Would the interviewee use it? During this stage, I was directly responsible for contacting industry members and conducting interviews.

Target Audience

We were able to determine that the app needed additional features to be useful. These needs, however, varied by user. In the end we realized that the two main groups who would most benefit from the app were a) small construction-related companies (contractors, painters, etc in companies of around 50 or fewer people) and b) homeowners wanting to head a DIY project or renovation.
