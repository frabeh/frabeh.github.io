At a Glance

  • Role: Project Manager / UX Researcher & Designer
  • Client: Gist Digital
  • Product: Accordion
  • Deliverables: Updated mobile app / new web application
  • Duration: June - September 2016

Overview

Gist Digital is a digital transformation company that incubates startups through its sister brand, Ignite. Accordion, one Ignite's incubees, is a collaboration platform designed to facilitate communication and document sharing for construction professionals.

I led a cross-functional team through iteration of the native mobile app and creation of an analogous web application.

My team was originally tasked with the development and design of a web application to match the existing native mobile app. I realized, however, that Accordion had been built according to unvalidated assumptions.

I worked with Gist Digital to redefine the scope so we could test these assumptions, and my team delivered user flows and wireframes based on our collected user feedback.

Challenge

Accordion’s flow was not intuitive, making simple tasks and team interactions difficult to accomplish. The app was also missing features necessary for a complete, easy-to-use experience.

Furthermore, Accordion’s intended audience— the construction industry— is segmented into a number of sectors with competing needs. We narrowed the intended audience and created a product with the tools and features that this audience needed.

Approach

We first spent a considerable time researching the industry through primary (interviews, demos) and secondary (articles, academic papers) research methods to determine product/market fit. It was essential for us to very clearly understand segments of the construction industry, their core pain points, and how or if Accordion was solving them.

Once we identified our target audience, we utilized our research and testing to align Accordion’s features list with our users' needs. We also researched how similar features had been designed in other products.

Market & Usability Research

We conducted 15-30 minute interviews and demos with multiple segments of the construction industry. I was responsible for creating an outline for the interview, shown below. Between the pre- and post-app questions, the interviewers asked our users to click through the app and narrate their reactions.

Our final sampling included scheduled and unscheduled interviews, interviews done online and in person, and were representative of not only different segments of the industry (for example, large and small companies with residential or commercial focus) but also different personnel within those segments involved in the final construction product (such as foremen, laborers, painters, plumbers, etc.).

We also conducted secondary market research by reviewing academic papers and articles about the construction industry’s current communication and documentation methods.

To improve the product’s usability, my team researched how Accordion’s key features are presented and used in successful applications such as Trello, F8, AirBnB, Slack, Google Hangouts, Google Docs, and Facebook Messenger.

This research also illuminated for us that Accordion was not adapted for either Android or iOS, and I instructed the team to optimize the app for both operating systems.

Information Architecture & User Flows

Users found Accordion’s information architecture and permanent navigation confusing. They were not able to easily switch between documents and chats for a single project. We modified the user flow to make the app more intuitive, nesting both Chats and Documents within the Project area.

I used this simplified overview to create the web app's information architecture. While the experience for both Documents and Chats are consistent on web and native mobile app, the web application’s static navigation bar changes the overall user flow slightly.

Reflection

This project beautifully demonstrated the importance of testing assumptions. Gist Digital came to us with a clear and defined project scope, but it was based on unvalidated hypotheses. I consider this project a success even though my team did not deliver everything requested in the original scope. Gist Digital, while still iterating on the product, can now be sure that Accordion is headed in the right direction.
